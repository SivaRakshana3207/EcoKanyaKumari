Discover the Soul of
Kanniyakumari

Named after the Hindu goddess Devi Kanniyakumari, this geographically blessed destination is where three major water bodies converge in a sacred Triveni Sangam.

From the historic Cape Comorin to a modern cultural hub, it’s the only spot in India to witness the sun setting and moon rising simultaneously.

Top Famous Foods You Must Try

Must Try

01

Villuppattu Most Popular

The "Bow Song" is a 1000-year-old storytelling art. A 7-foot bow is used to narrate stories of heroic local deities.

📅 Next Event: Temple Festivals (Mar-May) ▶ Watch on YouTube
02

Karakattam

An ancient folk dance where performers balance decorated bronze pots (Karakam) on their heads while dancing to the Thavil and Nadaswaram.

📅 Next Event: Mandaikadu Kodai (March) ▶ Watch on YouTube
03

Oyilattam

The "Dance of Grace." Men dance in a row with colored handkerchiefs, keeping rhythm with bells on their ankles (Salangai).

Planning Your Trip

When to Visit Kanyakumari

Kanyakumari is an all-year-round destination, but every season tells a different story.

Recommended
☀️

Oct - Mar

Peak Season

Expect: Pleasant weather with significantly lower humidity and clear blue skies.

You'll Love: Perfect conditions for beach hopping, ferry rides, and the Cape Comorin Festival.

🌤️

Apr - Jun

Moderate Season

Expect: Warm tropical days. Great for those who enjoy the vibrant summer sun.

You'll Love: Watching the spectacular Chithra Pournami (Sun and Moon facing each other).

🌧️

Jul - Sep

Off-Season

Expect: Refreshing monsoon rains and lush green landscapes throughout the district.

You'll Love: The dramatic clouds over the ocean and the gushing waterfalls at their fullest.

🎉 Local Highlight

Don't miss the Cape Comorin Festival in October—a 3-day cultural extravaganza!
